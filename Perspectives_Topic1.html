Data Preparation

I define data preparation as making data one currently possesses ready for appropriate statistical modeling. To me, its importance to proper statistical modeling can not be understated. While running suitable final models comes down primarily to understanding what models are needed to be run; successful data preparation requires tedious, careful attention to detail for numerous tasks—including but limited to:  

  • Restructuring the dataset
  • Handling missing data
  • Removing data
  • Transforming variables
  • Running preliminary analyses

Inadequacy in any of the above tasks results in final models that can not be trusted—and, because of that, leads to key recommendations that can not be trusted. Accordingly, I spend most of the time directly working with data in the data preparation stage.
